about me

I was born and raised in San Francisco. I've lived in SF, Amsterdam, DC, and Oxford, and I hold dual Dutch-American citizenship.

I work in Product & Analytics at the Interaction Company of California, makers of poke.com. I hold an MSc with Distinction from the University of Oxford in Social Science of the Internet, and a B.S.H. from Stanford University in Symbolic Systems (Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence), with a minor in Poetry.

At Oxford, I studied the ideological evolution of Silicon Valley, with a focus on effective accelerationism and the political stakes of the AGI race. Prior to that, I worked as a Business Analyst at McKinsey & Company in Washington, D.C. (2022–2024), advising clients across sectors on strategic AI deployment.

I continue to freelance with the Deliberative Democracy Lab at Stanford: most recently as an expert panelist on a Stanford × DeepMind × CloseUp panel on "Youth Perspectives on GenAI" (July 2025), and a moderator for a "National Deliberative Poll on AI Policies" (September 2025).

elsewhere

I study the Mesopotamian and Assyrian empires, contribute to citizen science projects on black holes, and try to read 52 books a year. I write poetry. I'm a Giants, Niners, and Warriors fan, with an affinity for the Premier League developed during my time at Oxford.

I spent a month in Patagonia in 2024. I run through Oxford's Uni Parks or Golden Gate Park, and play tennis whenever the weather cooperates.
